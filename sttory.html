How I Found My Way In

Origins

Hyderabad, India. Early 2000s. Born into a city where biryani is a religion, traffic is a boss fight, and every family gathering eventually turns into a discussion about your future.

A computer showed up in my life before I understood what it could do. Games got my attention. The internet kept it.

Years later, curiosity turned into tabs. Tabs turned into tutorials. Tutorials turned into unfinished projects, finished projects, and the occasional bug that somehow fixed itself after a restart.

Somewhere along the way, I stopped asking:

"How does this website work?"

and started asking:

"Can I build one better?"

School, Sports & Competition

School was only part of the story. Outside the classroom, I spent years training, competing, and testing my limits.

MMA and boxing became a huge part of my life. Long hours in the gym taught me discipline, consistency, and how to keep moving forward even when things weren't going my way.

Those lessons would later become just as useful in technology as they were in sports.

The Turning Point

COVID changed the plan.

What felt like setbacks became the push I needed to rethink my direction and start building something new.

I traded routine for curiosity, diving into code, cybersecurity, web development, digital marketing, and business.

Every skill opened the door to the next.

Today, I'm building websites, studying full stack development, exploring AI and cybersecurity, growing Code XMoRE, and working toward projects like BrickBuddy.

Still the same Hyderabad kid. Just chasing bigger challenges now. 🚀
